ACADEMIC PUBLICATIONS

A Computer Vision-Based Physical Activity Application for Children with Autism

IEEE Conference Paper Published

This paper introduces a computer vision-based physical activity application designed to support motor, social, and cognitive development in children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). Leveraging real-time motion tracking through Google MediaPipe and Unity, the system creates interactive virtual characters that mirror users' movements, promoting engagement through game-based learning. The platform incorporates AI-powered feedback, emotion recognition, and body tracking to offer a low-cost, accessible solution for use in classrooms and at home. By fostering active participation, the system encourages independence, socialization, and physical well-being among children with ASD.

A Digitalized Business Wargame Model for Foresight-based Future Planning and Decision-Making

IEEE Conference Paper Published

This paper presents a foresight-driven digital business wargaming model that integrates artificial intelligence, natural language processing, and interactive simulations to support strategic decision-making under uncertainty. By combining scenario-based planning with predictive analytics and role-based simulations, the system enables users to evaluate long-term impacts, enhance future thinking capabilities, and navigate complex competitive environments with greater adaptability and insight.
